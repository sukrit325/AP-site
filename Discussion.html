Discussion

Semiconductor performance depends on material properties such as bandgap energy, carrier mobility, thermal conductivity, and critical electric field, which influence efficiency, switching speed, voltage capability, and heat tolerance. As power density and speed demands rise, wide-bandgap materials like Silicon Carbide (SiC) and Gallium Nitride (GaN) outperform traditional silicon. With bandgaps around 3.2–3.4 eV (vs. 1.1 eV for silicon), they reduce leakage and operate efficiently at high temperatures. SiC offers high thermal conductivity and strong breakdown strength, making it ideal for electric vehicle inverters and high-voltage systems, though at higher cost. GaN provides high electron mobility and fast switching for RF devices, 5G, and compact chargers, despite thermal challenges. Gallium arsenide (GaAs) excels in microwave and optoelectronic applications but is less suited for high power. Silicon remains dominant due to cost and manufacturing maturity.
