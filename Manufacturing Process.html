Materials Required

  • Silicon wafer
  • Pure Germanium wafer
  • Gallium Arsenide wafer
  • Doping materials (Boron, Phosphorus)
  • Metal contacts (Aluminium)
  • Chemicals for cleaning (Acids, distilled water)

Equipments

  • Furnace (for heating)
  • Multimeter
  • Power supply
  • Thermometer
  • Microscope
  • Clean room gloves and mask

Method

  • Take equal size wafers of Silicon, Germanium, and Gallium Arsenide.
  • Clean the wafers properly.
  • Do doping processes (add impurities to control conductivity).
  • Make a simple diode or transistor from each material.
  • Connect to power supply.

Measure:

  • Current flow
  • Voltage
  • Temperature effect
  • Speed of switching
  • Record all readings.

    • Observation & result

    Property Silicon Germanium Gallium Arsenide
    Band Gap Medium(1.1eV) Low(0.6eV) High(1.63eV)
    Conductivity Moderate High Very High
    Heat Resistance High Low Medium
    Speed Moderate Low Very High
    Cost Low Medium High
    • Silicon is best for general electronic devices because it is cheap and heat resistant.
    • Germanium conducts electricity more easily but cannot handle high temperature.
    • Arsenide works very fast and is used in high-frequency and space devices.
    • For normal electronics → Silicon is best.
    • For high speed and microwave devices → Gallium Arsenide is best.
    • Germanium is rarely used now.
