  • Nitric acid (HNO₃)
  • Hydrofluoric acid (HF)

Equipment Required

Furnace

High-temperature heating for diffusion and annealing processes

Temperature Range: 900-1200°C

Multimeter

Measurement of voltage, current, and resistance

Accuracy: ±0.5%

Power Supply

Controlled electrical supply for device testing

Output: 0-100V, 0-10A

Thermometer

Temperature monitoring during heating processes

Range: -50 to 300°C

Microscope

Inspection of wafer surfaces and device structures

Magnification: 100x - 1000x

Safety Equipment

Clean room suit, gloves, and mask for protection

ISO Class 6 Cleanroom

Experimental Procedure

1

Wafer Preparation

Select equal-size wafers of Silicon, Germanium, and Gallium Arsenide with standardized dimensions.

2

Cleaning Process

Clean wafers thoroughly using acid solutions and distilled water to remove contaminants and surface oxides.

Rinse with dilute acid Distilled water wash Dry with nitrogen
3

Doping Process

Add controlled impurities (Boron for p-type, Phosphorus for n-type) to create junctions.

High-temperature diffusion Thermal annealing Depth control
4

Device Fabrication

Create simple diodes or transistors from each semiconductor material.

Pattern definition Metallization Contact formation
5

Device Testing

Connect devices to power supply and measure electrical characteristics.

6

Measurement & Analysis

Record comprehensive data on device performance and characteristics.

Silicon (Si)

Best for: General electronics and computing devices

  • ✓ Cost-effective
  • ✓ Excellent thermal stability
  • ✓ Mature manufacturing
  • ✓ Industry standard

Germanium (Ge)

Specialization: Infrared detectors and sensors

  • ✓ High conductivity
  • ✓ IR transparent
  • ✗ Temperature sensitive
  • ✗ Rarely used today

Gallium Arsenide (GaAs)

Best for: High-speed and optoelectronic devices

  • ✓ Fastest switching
  • ✓ Direct band gap
  • ✓ 5G & Microwave
  • ✓ Light emission

Practical Recommendations

1

Consumer Electronics: Silicon is the clear choice for CPUs, memory, and microcontrollers due to cost and reliability.

2

High-Speed Applications: Gallium Arsenide excels in radar, 5G communications, and microwave systems.

3

Optoelectronics: GaAs is essential for LEDs, lasers, and fiber-optic communications.

4

Specialized Sensing: Germanium infrared detectors remain valuable for thermal imaging applications.
